FAQ

Here are the most frequently asked questions about the club and esports!

Gaming

What is CSUF Gaming and Esports about?

CSUF Gaming & Esports hopes to offer a welcoming environment for both casual and competitive gamers at Cal State Fullerton to socialize with one another. Each of our meetings consist of a theme: for example, FPS, Nintendo, Horror, etc. depending on the day of the meeting.

When does the club meet?

We meet every other Friday from 6 - 9pm on our Discord server!

What games are played during the meetings?

The club has game nights that include all genres from FPS to board games. Although we currently have a semester schedule for each game night already, the club is always open to new suggestions and we encourage people to play games within the voice channels at any time.

What does membership include?

Membership grants access to the Paid Member role in our discord, various virtual tours that will take place throughout the semester, member giveaways, and a club t-shirt.

Do I need to pay for membership if I want to be and maintain membership status?

Paid membership is not required to be a part of the club!

How do I pay for membership?

We are currently finding other means of offering paid membership to those interested. Those who paid for memberships last semester will retain membership status.

Do I need to participate during game nights to maintain my membership status?

Participation during the game nights is completely voluntary and is not required to maintain membership status.

Esports

What are the official teams at Cal State Fullerton?

CSUF officially offers teams for Overwatch, Valorant, League of Legends, and Rocket League.

How many teams are there?

We have one Varsity team per game, and club teams can be made based on interest.

What do I need to be eligible for CSUF Esports?

In order to participate in CSUF Esports, you must be a full-time student with a minimum of 12 units for the semester, in addition to maintaining good standing with the campus (minimum 2.0 GPA). Incoming freshman do not require a GPA.

Can I still try out after the team has been formed?

If you wish to join an existing team, contact the appropriate team manager with your rank and availability.

Can I represent CSUF Esports if I am not on a team?

Being a part of CSUF Esports requires players to sign a contract. Any student can participate in the same tournaments as the Esports teams, but cannot represent CSUF Esports without signing said contract.

Why don't you have a team for [insert game]?

More than likely, if we don't have a team for a game, it is because the game either doesn't have enough interest or there hasn't been any initiative to start one.

I want to make a team for a game that isn't featured at CSUF. What do I do?

In order to start a team for a new game, there needs to be enough interest from players to start a team. Then, interested parties must schedule a meeting with the board. After getting approved, tryouts will be conducted and the team will be finalized after creating an official roster.
